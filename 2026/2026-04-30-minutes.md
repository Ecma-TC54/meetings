## 4 Key discussion

### ENISA and CycloneDX

Steve provided an update regarding ongoing discussions with ENISA related to Secure by Design / Secure by Default guidance documentation. CycloneDX attestations were referenced extensively in the current draft documentation, demonstrating increasing visibility and relevance of TC54 work within European cybersecurity initiatives.

Additional discussions focused on:

* Operationalization of CycloneDX attestations
* Assessor Studio open-source project <https://assessors.studio/>
* Support for CRA-related requirements
* Small and medium-sized business awareness of CRA obligations.

It was noted that TC54 and related work had also been referenced in ENISA materials.

### OWASP / OpenEoX Blog

A previously published OWASP/OpenEoX blog post covering OpenEoX and Common Lifecycle Enumeration (CLE) was referenced and shared with participants.

<https://owasp.org/blog/2026/04/15/end-of-life-cle-and-openeox.html>

### CRA and Standards Alignment

Discussion highlighted the growing relevance of CycloneDX and related TC54 specifications within European CRA implementation efforts and associated standards development activities. Interest in formal standardization and broader international alignment continues to increase.

### Future Approvals and Upcoming Work

* A public RFC related to CDXA fingerprints is currently open and scheduled for vote during the next TC54 meeting
* June and July are expected to involve significant specification review and approval activity
* Pre-reading of materials prior to meetings may become necessary to support efficient discussion and voting processes.

The related RFC link was shared with participants:

<https://github.com/CycloneDX/specification/pull/903>

### ISO/IEC JTC 1 Fast track submission preparations

* engagement with SC27 and related standardization groups.

### Working Group Updates

#### TG1 – Transparency Exchange API (TEA) (Olle Johansson)

Progress updates:

* Planning for TEA community release preparation
* Ongoing work on pagination consistency across specifications
* Major current focus on authentication and authorization models
* Integration of Common Lifecycle Enumeration (CLE) concepts within TEA
* Demonstrations of cross-implementation interoperability activities.

The TEA specification continues to evolve toward broader operational readiness and interoperability support.

#### TG2 – Package URL (PURL) (Philippe Ombredanne)

Steady progress across several areas:

* Strong industry interest and visibility following Eclipse Open Source conference participation in Brussels
* Continued work on formal grammar development for PURL
* Increased engagement from the Conda community
* Growing usage and adoption metrics for PURL-related libraries
* Ongoing work related to versioning and package management interoperability
* Downloads: <https://pypistats.org/packages/packageurl-python>.

The group continues bi-weekly committee calls and active specification development.

#### TG3 – Lifecycle / Events (Steve Springett)

Continued progress:

* Publication and visibility of CLE-related materials
* Requests for additional lifecycle event types
* Integration work between CLE and TEA
* Continued implementation demonstrations and interoperability activities.

CLE support within TEA was described as operationally integrated and actively progressing.

#### TG4 – Sustainability (Chris de Almeida and Piotr P. Karwasz)

Work is ongoing, discussions focused primarily on operational matters related to meeting infrastructure and collaboration tools.

Topics discussed:

* Challenges with meeting hosting and recording workflows
* Potential use of shared accounts or alternate collaboration platforms
* Trade-offs between Zoom, Microsoft Teams, Slack Huddles, and other approaches.

The group agreed to continue evaluating practical solutions while maintaining current operations.
