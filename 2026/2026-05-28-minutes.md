## 4 Key discussion

### CycloneDX Specification Updates

The committee agreed to take a decision at this meeting following the completion of the RFC review period in CycloneDX, which included feedback gathered through Slack and the mailing list. The proposal adds support for a broader set of existing hash algorithms used for fingerprinting. Members confirmed unanimous support for allowing custom fingerprints to be added to the CBOM, with no objections raised. As a next step, the item will be placed on the agenda and an issue will be created to track the work.

### Future Technical Reviews

Committee was informed that several significant proposals are expected for review during upcoming meetings, including:

* Blueprint modeling work.
* Threat modeling enhancements.
* Additional CycloneDX 2.0 activities.

Pre-reading materials will be distributed in advance to facilitate review.

### Community Outreach

The committee discussed opportunities to improve communication with the broader community regarding TC54 activities and progress. Members expressed support for increasing visibility through periodic updates and outreach activities.

### AI Transparency and Regulatory Developments

The committee discussed recent industry efforts related to AI transparency, including published minimum-element frameworks and regulatory developments.

Updates included:

* Ongoing collaboration with industry stakeholders.
* CycloneDX work related to AI models, AI systems, and software supply chain transparency.
* Development of guidance intended to support emerging regulatory requirements.
* Planning for significant AI-related enhancements within CycloneDX 2.0.

### CycloneDX Assessor Studio

An update was provided regarding CycloneDX Assessor Studio and machine-readable attestations. A webinar is being planned to introduce the tool and demonstrate its capabilities to the broader community.

### European Regulatory Initiatives

Members discussed upcoming European Commission initiatives related to:

* Cloud and AI development.
* Digital sovereignty.
* Open-source software support.
* Supply chain security.
* Semiconductor and infrastructure initiatives.

The committee noted the potential relevance of these activities to future TC54 work.

### Working Group Updates

#### TG1 – Transparency Exchange API (TEA) (Olle Johansson, reported by Pavel)

The group reported:

* Completion of pagination-related work.
* Progress in transforming existing markdown documentation.
* Development of a documentation generation process from OpenAPI specifications to Ecma publication formats.
* Ongoing discussions regarding authentication and authorization architecture.
* ApSec to be in Vienna in June, session together to discuss authentication for API.

#### TG2 – Package URL (PURL) (Philippe Ombredanne)

Steady progress across several areas, the group continues bi-weekly committee calls and active specification development.

The committee held an initial discussion on potential future work related to services, SaaS, APIs, and broader software supply chain identification.

#### TG3 – Lifecycle / Events (Steve Springett)

The group continues work on software package change tracking, including:

* License change identification.
* Ecosystem adoption considerations.
* Tooling support for implementation.

CLE support within TEA was described as operationally integrated and actively progressing.

#### TG4 – Sustainability (Chris de Almeida and Piotr P. Karwasz)

The group reported continued development of classifications describing project maintenance and lifecycle states.

Work includes:

* Mapping common open-source project statuses.
* Defining simplified consumer-facing indicators.
* Preparing initial specification drafts for committee review.
