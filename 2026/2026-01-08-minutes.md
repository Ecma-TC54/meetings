## 4. Key discussion
The FOSDEM event was announced for 30 January – 1 February in Brussels, including
AboutCode workshop on PURL and SBOM followed by a pre‑dinner gathering to recognize the
TC54 Standards approvals. The event on Friday 30 January will be co-sponsored by Ecma.
Steve, Olle, and Philippe will be in attendance along with other TC54 delegates. Invitation will
be posted for the broader open source community. Invitation will also be posted on Slack,
LinkedIn and event platform.

Steve Springett reported on CycloneDX 2.0 status, noting that several new features will be
added. After the community review process, there will be a formal TC54 review and this is
expected by next month.

The anticipated target is based on the OWASP release date in July-August, then a broader TC54
committee review before an Ecma GA approval in December. The TC54 community is
encouraged to follow updates in the Slack channel for the latest information.
There was a discussion about using CycloneDX 2.0 and TEA to better reach implementers, with
Steve and Starr coordinating efforts. OWASP AppSec Vienna is in June 2026, and was identified
as an ideal venue for this outreach, plus there may be opportunities for Ecma to also collaborate
in the event. 

__Update from TC54-TG1 (Olle Johansson or Chair) Transparency exchange API__
- The 1.0 release is moving forward, and organizational certification has been added. By the
next meeting it is expected to have more clarity on the timelines.
- The next ExeCom is scheduled for 31 March – 1 April, and the 1.0 release will need to meet
the required 60 day review period before approval.

__Update from TC54-TG2 (Philippe Ombredanne or Chair) Package URL__
- Work is currently underway on the testing schema.
- The PackageURL.org website continues to make strong progress.
- The group is preparing for upcoming discussions on the VERS specification, and ongoing
efforts are focused on repository cleanup and ensuring full alignment with Ecma
documentation format.
- It was also noted that the PURL schema has not yet been registered with IANA, though a
ticket is already in place.

__Update from TC54-TG3 (Jordan Harband) CLE__
The group is looking forward to VERS becoming standardized so CLE can be updated
accordingly.
- There is continued interest in identifying opportunities for CLE to be adopted in more places,
as well as in supporting both the production and consumption of CLE information.
- OpenSSF has expressed interest in the CLE specification, Olle and Jordan will coordinate
further engagement.
- Interoperability remains an important goal, including the ability to produce an OpenEoX
document from a CLE document.
- Work within TG3 will continue, although there is no definitive roadmap at this stage and no
immediate plans to request anything from the ExeCom or GA.

__Update from TC54- TG4 (Chris de Almeida and Piotr P. Karwasz) Sustainability Group__
- Work continues, TG4 repos needs more details.
- OpenSSF has interest, to check:
  https://github.com/ossf/wg-vulnerability-disclosures/issues/175
  Process reminder was noted that all Task Groups shall be listed on the agenda for every
  meeting, and TGs must formally report. Any changes in TG convenorship must be approved by
  committee consensus

__New Business__
- The group agreed to keep the existing Zoom link and maintain the monthly meeting cadence.
- Steve agreed to flag upcoming issues, particularly changes related to CycloneDX 2.0, in the
private TC54 Slack channel ahead of meetings so participants can prepare in advance.
- It was noted that the TC39 conducts annual elections for conveners, editors, and spec
writers, and suggested defining a similar process for TC54. This will be added to the agenda
for the next TC meeting, where individuals will be asked whether they wish to continue in
their roles.
- Piotr and Chris have already confirmed they would accept their TG4 appointments.
- The group also discussed improving community updates from TC54, including the idea of
recording a short video, providing quarterly updates from each task group and the core
TC54, and having conveners along with Steve for the core, deliver these updates.
